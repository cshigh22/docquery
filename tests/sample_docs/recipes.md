# Kimchi Stew (Kimchi Jjigae) Recipes

Kimchi Jjigae is a staple in Korean cuisine—a comforting, spicy, and savory stew that makes the most out of older, heavily fermented kimchi. Here are two of the most popular variations: a classic pork belly version and a quick tuna version.

---

## 1. Classic Pork Belly Kimchi Stew 

The rich fat from the pork belly perfectly balances the sharp, acidic tang of the fermented kimchi. 

**Prep time:** 10 mins | **Cook time:** 20 mins | **Servings:** 2

### Ingredients

**For the Stew:**
*   1 ½ cups well-fermented (sour) kimchi, cut into bite-sized pieces
*   ½ lb (about 200g) pork belly, cut into ½-inch thick bite-sized slices
*   ½ block firm tofu, cut into ½-inch thick rectangles
*   ½ medium onion, sliced
*   1 green onion, cut into 1-inch pieces
*   2 cups water or anchovy/kelp stock

**For the Seasoning Base:**
*   1 Tbsp gochugaru (Korean red chili flakes)
*   1 Tbsp soy sauce
*   1 tsp gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
*   1 tsp minced garlic
*   ¼ cup kimchi brine (the juice from your kimchi container)
*   1 tsp sesame oil

### Instructions

1.  **Prepare the Base:** In a small bowl, mix the gochugaru, soy sauce, gochujang, minced garlic, and kimchi brine to create your seasoning paste.
2.  **Sauté:** Place a medium-sized pot (or traditional Korean stone pot) over medium heat. Add the pork belly slices and cook for about 3-5 minutes until they begin to release their fat and brown slightly. 
3.  **Add Kimchi:** Add the chopped kimchi and the sliced onions to the pot. Sauté together with the pork for another 3-4 minutes until the kimchi softens.
4.  **Boil:** Pour in the 2 cups of water (or stock) and stir in your seasoning base. Bring the pot to a boil over medium-high heat. 
5.  **Simmer:** Once boiling, reduce the heat to medium-low, cover, and let it simmer for 10 to 15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld and the pork to become tender. 
6.  **Finish:** Add the sliced tofu and green onions to the top of the stew. Simmer for an additional 3-5 minutes. Drizzle with the sesame oil just before turning off the heat. Serve hot with a bowl of steamed white rice.
